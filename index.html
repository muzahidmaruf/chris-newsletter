The Thing No One Tells You

There are two economies. You were only taught one.

The application. The credential. The interview. That's the visible economy, and it's maybe 30% of how outcomes actually get decided.

30%

The visible economy

The posting, the resume, the org chart, the rules written down. The part everyone can see is the part everyone is already fighting over.

70%

The invisible economy

The conversation before you were considered. The room where your name came up, or didn't. The relationships, the timing, and the unwritten rules that decide who gets the offer, the seat, the deal.

The people who learn to see the system move differently. That is what this letter is about.

A Bit About Me

I've watched the same system run from inside three different worlds.

For 11 years I climbed the higher-ed ladder, on track to a university presidency. Then I walked away to break into Big Tech, not by applying online and waiting, but by running a gauntlet: 20 interviews, 5 roles, 6 months. It ended in a life-changing Solution Engineer role at Salesforce.

Higher ed. Career. Tech. Three different worlds, and the same hidden machinery underneath all of them. Once you see how decisions actually get made in one, you start seeing it everywhere. That pattern is the invisible economy, and learning to read it is what bought me freedom, proximity to my family, and impact at scale.

From there I led Salesforce's HBCU engagement strategy and worked alongside Disney, Netflix, Apple, and the White House. Now I write down what I learned the hard way, so you don't have to learn it 10 years too late.
